# Legal Documents Guide

## Overview of the Legal Documentation Lifecycle
In digital forensics, legal documentation is crucial for maintaining the integrity and legitimacy of the investigation process. This guide will outline the various types of legal documents used throughout the lifecycle of a digital forensics investigation.

## Document Types
1. **Search Warrant**: A legal document that authorizes law enforcement to conduct a search of a location.
2. **Arrest Warrant**: A legal document that authorizes the arrest of an individual.
3. **Subpoena**: A document ordering a person to attend a court or produce documents.
4. **Media Release**: A legal document that communicates information to the public regarding an investigation.
5. **Chain of Custody Documentation**: Records that track the handling of evidence.

## Document Generation
- **Search Warrant**: Typically generated by law enforcement officers with a judge's approval.
- **Arrest Warrant**: Prepared by law enforcement and issued by a magistrate.
- **Subpoena**: Prepared by an attorney or legal authority.
- **Media Release**: Created by the public relations officer or designated spokesperson.
- **Chain of Custody**: Maintained by investigators handling evidence.

## Key Information
Each document includes specific key fields:
- **Search Warrant**: Location, description of evidence, time frame.
- **Arrest Warrant**: Allegations, identity of the suspect.
- **Subpoena**: Court details, required documents or appearance dates.
- **Media Release**: Information about the investigation, statements, and guidelines for media interaction.
- **Chain of Custody**: Details of evidence handlers, locations, times, and conditions of storage.

## Relation to Mobile Forensics
These documents ensure that the processes followed in mobile forensics labs comply with legal standards to maintain the admissibility of evidence in court.
